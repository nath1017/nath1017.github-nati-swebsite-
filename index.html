Lessons Learned

In this section, I will share my 5 learnings from each lesson.

1. Infographics

One important lesson I learned from studying the history of vacuum tube computers is the incredible impact these early technologies had on shaping the future of computing. This also helped me appreciate how technological advancements have evolved over time, making computing much more accessible and powerful.

2. Cybercrime Case

In studying the Philippine cybercrime case involving trafficking in Cebu, I learned about the severe and often hidden dangers of cybercrime. I also realized the vital role law enforcement plays in identifying and dismantling such networks. The case was a stark reminder of the dark side of the internet and how much work is needed to ensure safe online environments, especially for vulnerable populations.

3. HTML First Activity

My first activity in HTML taught me a lot about web development. I found it to be both fun and challenging, especially when learning the structure and syntax of HTML tags. Although I initially struggled with concepts like element nesting and properly linking styles and content, by the end of the activity, I felt more confident in creating basic web pages and more motivated to explore further into web development.

4. Wireframe

Creating my first wireframe for a portfolio was an eye-opening experience. I realized how crucial wireframing is in planning the layout and user experience of a website. It helped me visualize how different elements should be organized and how users would interact with the content.

5. Technology Trends

Learning about current technology trends such as cloud computing, AI, and the Internet of Things (IoT) has broadened my understanding of how interconnected and innovative the modern tech landscape has become. These trends have shown me the immense potential of technology to create efficiencies, drive innovation, and change the way we live and work.

My Favorite/Challenging Lesson

My favorite and most challenging lesson was learning HTML and CSS. I found it exciting because it gave me the tools to create and design websites from scratch, allowing me to bring my ideas to life visually. However, it was also challenging, especially when it came to understanding how to properly structure HTML and use CSS to style elements in a way that was both functional and visually appealing. I struggled with concepts like layout techniques, responsiveness, and ensuring cross-browser compatibility, but as I practiced, I began to see improvement. This lesson not only enhanced my technical skills but also sparked my interest in web design and development, making me eager to learn more.
